Oahu’s North Shore is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the island’s beauty, local charm, and hidden gems. Known around the world for its massive winter surf, the North Shore beaches also transform into calm, crystal-clear water during the summer months, perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking. The North Shore is also known for its iconic food spots located along the coastline. Located about an hour drive from the airport, the North Shore provides an escape from the busier touristy areas, allowing you to relax and enjoy your adventures at your own pace. The North Shore is packed with experiences waiting to be had by adventurers of all kinds.

#### **Summer Water Adventures**

During the summer months, North Shore beaches are incredible for swimming and sunset watching. Check out some of our favorites:

- Waimea beach is a popular spot because of its crystal clear-water you can bob waves in, and the iconic rock you can jump off of.

- Paddleboard or Kayak down Anahulu River in Haleiwa. Glide along turtles, jump off Rainbow Bridge, and swing on ropes wings, all with incredible views on this one-of-a-kind adventure.

- Watch the sunset at Sunset Beach after grabbing some Acai Bowls from Banzai Bowls across the street.

- Snorkel at Shark’s Cove, which is protected by the rocks so that you can explore the tide pools year round.

- Snorkel at Turtle Bay

#### **Winter Activities**

During the winter the North Shore transforms into one of the most popular surf spots in the world. With its famous surf competitions and big waves, it’s something you won’t want to miss!

- Watch the surfers at Sunset Beach, Waimea, or Banzai Pipeline.

- Visit Haleiwa Town and explore the different shops and eateries in this small surf town. 

- Check out hikes like Kaena Point trail or the Ehukai Pillbox Trail for epic views of the North Shore.

- Paddleboard alongside turtles at Kawela Bay. This bay offers calm waters perfect for kayaking or paddleboarding with the chance to spot Hawaii’s unique sea life.

#### **Best Places to Eat on the North Shore**

It’s no secret the North Shore is home to some of the best foodie spots on the island. Here are some of our favorites:

- The Kahuku Food Trucks: From burgers at Seven Brothers to the famous shrimp trucks, to shave ice and treats, the Kahuku food trucks offer something for everyone. The food trucks offer a fun social scene with lots of dining options and plenty of outdoor seating. It’s a spot you won’t want to miss!

- Banzai Bowls: Arguably the best Acai Bowls and smoothies on the island, Banzai bowls has two locations on the North Shore, one across from Sunset Beach and one in Haleiwa. They’re blended smoothie bowls loaded with fresh fruit toppings make them a perfect refreshing treat while you are exploring.

- Matsumoto’s Shave Ice: One of the most iconic shave ice locations on Oahu is Matsumoto’s, known for its perfectly soft shave ice and variety of flavors, it often has a line out the door with eager customers. It is located right in the middle of Haleiwa, so you can stop in while exploring the shops.

- However if you want to skip the line and get just as good shave ice, check out Aoki’s Shave Ice located right across the street!

- Ted’s Bakery: Another famous food spot on Oahu is Ted’s Bakery–most famously known for their Chocolate Haupia Pie. They also serve local favorite plates and a variety of bakery items.

- Kua’Aina Sandwich and Burgers: A great burger spot with generous portions. Featuring Ahi sandwiches, some of the best burgers on the island, and thin french fries, this is the perfect stop after a day spent at the beach.
