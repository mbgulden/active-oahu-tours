Tucked away on Oahu’s West Side lies a hidden gem known as Electric Beach or more properly referred to as Kahe Point. Often overlooked by tourists, this vibrant coastal spot offers a unique and thrilling snorkel experience. Warm water from a nearby powerplant attracts an incredible variety of sealife, often visited by sea turtles, tropical fish, and even the occasional pod of dolphins. Join us for a guided tour or rent out some snorkel gear with Active Oahu and dive into this unforgettable underwater world. In this guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know to plan the perfect adventure and create memories that will last a lifetime.

#### **About Electric Beach**

Electric Beach is a local favorite located just North of Ko’Olina. It’s about a 45 minute drive from Waikiki and 30 minutes from the North Shore, it is accessible by car or the city bus. It got its nickname from the HECO powerplant which uses a pipe that extends into the ocean to cool down the plant. As a result, it shoots out warm water into the ocean, creating a unique microenvironment that attracts an impressive array of sealife. You won’t find anywhere quite like Electric Beach on Oahu.

#### **What to Expect**

Your adventure at Electric Beach begins by parking at Kahe Point Beach Park, where you’ll find amenities including bathrooms, an outdoor shower to rinse off, and a lifeguard stand, giving you an added layer of reassurance as you get ready to dive in the clear ocean waters and explore. The beach itself may be small, but it is unmatched when it comes to natural beauty and underwater excitement. 
Be aware that the shoreline can have a powerful shorebreak depending on the conditions. Due to the current and shorebreak, snorkeling at Electric Beach is best suited for confident swimmers and experienced snorkelers.The reef lies approximately 100-200 yards from shore, so be prepared to swim for an extended period of time as you experience the magical underwater experience. Once you reach the reef, you are in for a snorkeling experience unlike any other. You’ll find yourself gliding along schools of colorful tropical fish, overlooking the reef in crystal-clear waters. If you are lucky, you may even spot sea turtles and dolphins! The best time of day for having the possibility of seeing dolphins is in the morning. 
As thrilling as it is to see these sea creatures up close, remember to respect their space. Avoid touching, chasing, or feeding any of the sea life, especially dolphins, which may approach you out of curiosity. These sea creatures come to the waters of Electric Beach to rest, so it is crucial to respect their space. Federal law states that you need to stay at least 50 yards away from dolphins and 10 feet away from sea turtles. Observing these creatures from a distance ensures they remain safe and wild. After exploring in the ocean, make a day of your trip to electric beach by hanging out on the beach, soaking up the sun rays with a cooler filled with snacks and drinks while you watch the waves roll in.

- Sunscreen

- Plenty of water
