## All of the Things to Do, See, and Experience at Kailua Beach Park

Kailua Beach Park has everything you need to enjoy an amazing day on Windward Oahu. It’s got the facilities (3 bathrooms and plenty of outdoor showers), lots of parking (tip: Come early for a spot), safety (2 lifeguard stands, small shore break, and reef-protected waters of Kailua Bay), and lots to do there (Kailua Beach itself merits a visit). When you combine that with chilling with a cooler full of drinks, paddleboarding in Kailua Bay while your kids play in the sand and surf, or even kayaking to the offshore islands, it’s easily a beach day that tops the charts and should be a priority on your Oahu itinerary.

Kailua Beach Park is adjacent to just a small portion of Kailua Beach. Kailua Beach actually covers 2.2 linear miles with as much variation as you could look for in a beach. Along that long stretch of world-class white sand beach, there are year-round surf spots, body boarding style waves, kiteboarding waypoints, kayak and canoe routes or just different variations of sand for your beachgoing experience. If you feel like you need some space, want to get the full Kailua Beach experience or just like “long walks on the beach,” take a cruise along the shoreline. Mornings boast amazing sunrises, especially when you factor in the offshore Mokulua Islands in the distance viewable from almost anywhere along Kailua Beach.

## Getting to Kailua Beach Park. The Trip is Worth It.

The best way to get to Kailua Beach Park is to rent a car or catch a ride on the bus or rideshare. The short of it is simple: from Honolulu, Kailua is only a 30 minute drive from Waikiki, and most major accommodations and 45 minutes from Ko’Olina Area. On your drive you’ll cross over one of Oahu’s most scenic highways, the Pali Highway on your way to Kailua, Oahu. Upon driving to Kailua, you’ll probably see active people out biking or running, and plenty of tasty eateries highlighted along your route. Kailua is an active and adventurous town. From there, Kailua Beach Park is just a short drive down through Kailua Town and to one of the two main parking lots of Kailua Beach Park.

### North Parking Lot of Kailua Beach Park

The north parking lot of Kailua Beach Park usually fills up last. You’ll do a lap around the roundabout and hang right to go into this parking area. After a few speed bumps, you’ll pass by the staging and launch area for kiteboarders with parking on either side all along the route. You’ll see one of Kailua Beach Park’s restrooms on the left with some more parking, then some parking on the right with a one-way exit to the street next to the bridge. The bridge and canal mark the launch area for canoes and kayaks. There’s usually some parking at the far end of the parking lot near that outlet to the street. Keep an eye out for active residents or beachgoers that are walking or riding along the beach bike path that goes along the whole length of Kailua Beach Park.

![View of the Kailua Beach Parking Lot](https://activeoahutours.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/DSC1502-2.jpg)

### South Parking Lot of Kailua Beach Park

The North Parking Lot of Kailua Beach Park usually fills up first. The South parking lot is more accessible since it has a more logical flow with one opening that serves as both the entrance and exit rather than the one-way route of the North Parking Lot. If you want to save yourself having to backtrack, just go to the North Parking Lot of Kailua Beach Park first and if that one happens to be full, try your luck at the South Parking Lot.

## Get Out & Kayak: Kayaking Trips from Kailua Beach Park

### Kayak to Popoia Island & in Kailua Bay

Popoia Island is a bird sanctuary located right off of Kailua beach. The island is a short 15-20 minute paddle, making it the perfect addition for adventurers of all levels! Once you land on the island you can walk around its perimeter and try to spot the nesting endangered birds. From Popoia island you can continue your journey around Kailua bay, or paddle to Lanikai beach, the Mokulua Islands, or simply back to Kailua Beach. Whether you are looking for a quick paddle, or an all-day ocean adventure, Kayaking from Kailua Beach Park offers something for everyone!

![View of Popoia Island](https://activeoahutours.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Screenshot-2025-04-28-at-3.18.38 PM.png)

![two people kayaking in the ocean at Kailua Beach](https://activeoahutours.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Active-Oahu-327.jpg)
##### Kayak to Lanikai Beach from Kailua Beach Park

Since there is no public parking directly in front of Lanikai beach, one of the most scenic and fun ways to get there is by kayak from Kailua Beach Park. After launching from Kailua Beach Park and getting into deeper water, you’ll head east and enjoy the view of the Mokulua Islands and stunning turquoise water as you paddle towards Lanikai beach. Once you land on the soft white sand, you’ll understand why Lanikai is one of Oahu’s most beloved beaches. Enjoy crystal-clear waters perfect for snorkeling, calm conditions and kid-sized waves ideal for families with young children, and picture perfect scenery all-around.

### Kayak to the Mokulua Islands from Kailua Beach Park

Looking for a longer adventure on the water? Paddle from Kailua Beach Park to the Mokulua Islands for the ultimate adventure! It takes about an hour to an hour and half to paddle to the islands depending on the ocean conditions and your pace. Once you reach the islands, the adventure continues on the land! Hike around the rocky perimeter, explore the tidepools, or snorkel in the crystal-clear waters around the shore. If you’re lucky, you may even spot some of Hawaii’s unique marine life, including Hawaiian Monk Seals and Green Sea Turtles!

## Get into some Shorebreak and Bodyboard

### Bodyboarding at Kaulia Beach

Kailua beach has calm waves, making it ideal for younger kids and families to try their hand at bodyboarding. The calm conditions provide a safe environment for beginners to experience the thrill of catching a wave while avoiding the danger of bigger waves. Bodyboarding at Kailua Beach is the perfect activity for gaining confidence in the water while enjoying one of Oahu’s most scenic beaches.

### Bodyboarding at Kalama Beach

Kalama beach is a local favorite for its laid-back vibe and ideal conditions and perfect waves for beginning bodyboarding. The mild to moderate rolling waves make ideal conditions for older kids and adults to have fun without the intensity of bigger surf breaks. Convenient parking, soft sand, and incredible views make it a top pick for an easygoing beach day. ## Additional Things to Do Nearby

### Lanikai Beach: One of the Most Famous Beaches in the World

Lanikai beach is known for it’s powdery white sand, crystal clear waters, and view of the Mokulua Islands. In addition to the beauty of this beach, its also a gateway to a variety of unforgettable adventures. This beach is known for its amazing snorkeling where you can see bright coral, a variety of fish, sea turtles, and other marine life. You can also make a day of your trip to Lanikai Beach and paddleboard or kayak out to the Mokulua Islands where you’ll find tide pools, amazing panoramic views of the Koolau mountains, and exceptional snorkeling along the shoreline. It’s also the perfect place to catch a sunrise as you walk along the beach. Lanikai Beach is world famous for a reason!

### Lanikai Pillbox Hike

The Lanikai pillbox hike offers some of the best views on Oahu! The 30-minute climb to the top rewards you with sweeping panoramic views of the turquoise water off of Lanikai beach and the iconic Mokulua Islands. At the summit you’ll discover pillboxes that were used for surveillance during World War II.
