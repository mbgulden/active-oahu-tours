#### **About the Sandbar**

So what exactly is the sandbar? The Kaneohe Sandbar is a naturally raised bed of sand located off of the Eastern Coast of Oahu. It is only accessible by boat, kayak, or paddleboard as it is too far to swim out to. Along the way there are coral reefs and places to free dive into the crystal clear waters. Once you reach the sandbar you can hop out of your kayak and experience the surreal sensation of standing up in shallow water surrounded by deep ocean. Depending on the tide, the water at the sandbar is typically ankle to knee-deep and even at high tide, the water usually isn’t higher than waist-deep. On clear, windless days, it’s like gliding on glass with how smooth and clear the water is and many visitors see turtles gliding alongside them as they paddle.

#### **Who Would Enjoy the Sandbar**

The Sandbar is about a 45 minute paddle out, making it ideal for those in good physical condition. Families also love this adventure, but keep in mind that constant supervision is essential due to the drop-off into deeper water surrounding the sandbar. Load up with snacks, drinks, and reef-safe sunscreen (available for purchase at our shop if you need to pick some up on your way) and make a day of hanging out at the sandbar and enjoying the water and views around you. The self-guided adventure typically takes four hours to complete, including driving to and from our shop with your gear, with the option to extend your adventure for a full-day.

#### **Enjoying the Sandbar**

You’ll begin by making the scenic 25 minute drive to the He’eia Kea Boat Harbor where you will find convenient parking and restrooms. When you are ready to begin your adventure, you’ll carry the kayaks over to the designated kayak launch location to the left of the parking lot. For your safety, it is important to launch from this location instead of the docks, which are reserved for larger boats. From the launch spot you can paddle at your own pace and explore the area on the way to the sandbar. We recommend getting out and snorkeling at the different reefs along the way. You’ll paddle southeast for about 45 minutes to reach the sandbar. Whenever you get out of the kayak, make sure to attach your floating rope to the kayak with the clip and wear the other end on your wrist/hand to keep your kayak with you at all times.
