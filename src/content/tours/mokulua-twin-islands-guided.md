## Mokulua Twin Islands Guided Kayak Tour

This is the one. The tour that put Active Oahu on the map. Paddle across Kailua Bay's turquoise waters to the iconic Mokulua Islands — Moku Nui and Moku Iki — with an expert local guide who knows every tide pool, every turtle hangout, and every hidden gem these islands have to offer.

### Why This Tour?

The Mokulua Islands are Hawaii's most photographed offshore islets for a reason — they're stunning from every angle. But photos don't capture the experience of paddling toward them across clear blue water, landing on a wild island, hiking to the summit, and snorkeling in water so clear it feels like an aquarium.

Our guides don't just lead — they educate. You'll learn about the geological forces that formed these islands, the native seabirds that nest here, the Hawaiian cultural significance of the area, and the marine ecosystem that makes Kailua Bay one of the healthiest reef environments on Oahu.

### What to Expect

**7:00 AM — Meet at Kailua Beach Park.** Your guide greets you at the boat ramp with hot coffee and a smile. You'll get fitted for gear, receive a comprehensive safety briefing, and practice a few paddle strokes in the shallows.

**7:30 AM — Launch.** Push off from one of the world's most beautiful beaches. The first 10 minutes are the adjustment period — finding your rhythm, getting comfortable with the kayak. The water inside the reef is calm and clear.

**8:15 AM — The Crossing.** The Mokulua Islands grow larger with every stroke. Your guide keeps the group together and points out marine life along the way. Turtles surface. Eagle rays glide beneath you. The Ko'olau Mountains frame the entire experience.

**8:45 AM — Land on Moku Nui.** Beach your kayak on the sandy shore of Moku Nui. Take a moment — you just paddled to an island in the middle of the Pacific Ocean.

**9:00 AM — Island Exploration.** Your guide leads a hike to the summit (steep but quick — 10 minutes) for 360° views of Kailua Bay, Lanikai, and the endless Pacific. Then down to Queen's Bath on the south side — a natural ocean-fed pool where you can cool off. Explore tide pools along the shoreline and learn about the island's ecology.

**10:00 AM — Snorkeling.** Back in the water. The area around Moku Nui has some of Kailua's best snorkeling — healthy coral, bold reef fish, and sea turtles that are completely unfazed by human visitors.

**11:00 AM — Return Paddle.** The wind is usually at your back on the return trip. You'll feel like a seasoned paddler now — confident, relaxed, taking in the views.

**11:45 AM — Back on Kailua Beach.** You did it. Your guide will help you haul the kayaks up and send you off with recommendations for the best post-paddle lunch spots in Kailua Town.

### Tour Schedule

- **Morning Tour:** 7:00 AM – 12:00 PM (recommended — calmest water, best light)

Kailua Beach Park boat ramp (near the kayak launch area). Free parking available. Detailed directions provided upon booking.
