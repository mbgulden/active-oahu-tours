## Popoia Island & Kailua Bay Guided Kayak Tour

Popoia Island — also known as Flat Island — is Kailua's best-kept secret. Just a short paddle from Kailua Beach Park, this protected seabird sanctuary offers world-class snorkeling, abundant marine life, and a peaceful island experience without the longer crossing to the Mokuluas.

### Why Choose Popoia?

Popoia gives you the island landing experience that makes Kailua kayaking famous, but with a shorter, easier paddle. It's the perfect choice for:

- **Families with younger kids** — no long open-water crossing
- **First-time kayakers** — protected waters, close to shore
- **Photographers** — the best angle of the Ko'olau Mountains from the water
- **Nature lovers** — seabird colony, turtle feeding grounds, healthy coral

### What to Expect

Your guide meets you at Kailua Beach Park boat ramp for gear fitting and a comprehensive safety briefing. After some practice strokes in the shallows, you'll push off and paddle toward Popoia Island — a relaxing 20–30 minute crossing in Kailua Bay's protected waters.

Once on the island, your guide will lead you around the shoreline, pointing out the wedge-tailed shearwater nesting burrows and explaining the island's role as a native seabird sanctuary. Then it's into the water for snorkeling — the rocky edges of Popoia are a magnet for Hawaiian green sea turtles and vibrant reef fish.

### Half-Day vs. Full-Day

- **Half-Day (4 hours):** Paddle to Popoia, explore the island, snorkel with turtles, paddle back. A perfect morning adventure.
- **Full-Day (5 hours):** All of the above, plus additional snorkeling time, a beach picnic on the island, and the option to circumnavigate Popoia for a different perspective.
